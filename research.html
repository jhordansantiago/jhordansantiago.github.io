Research Interests

My research focuses on the fundamental aspects of quantum mechanics through the lens of quantum optics. I am particularly interested in understanding how quantum systems behave in interferometric setups and how we can characterize their non-classical properties through phase-space representations.

Current Projects

Quantum Interference and Photon Statistics — Investigating the statistical properties of photonic states subjected to addition and subtraction operations in Mach-Zehnder interferometers. This work explores how single-photon manipulations affect quantum interference patterns and photon counting distributions.

Wigner Functions of Photonic States — Studying the Wigner function formalism as a tool for characterizing quantum states in phase space. This research examines decorrelated photonic states and their representation through quasi-probability distributions.

Quantum Entanglement in Optical Systems — Analyzing the statistical behavior of entangled photonic states in interferometric configurations, with emphasis on understanding correlations and quantum coherence properties.
